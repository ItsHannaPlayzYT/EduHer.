What is STEM?

STEM stands for Science, Technology, Engineering, and Maths. It is a learning style that combines these four subjects so that students are better able to understand the world. Learning through STEM enables students to use their hands and solve everyday issues. Learning with STEM enables students to think critically and creatively and readies them for future careers in healthcare, engineering, and technology. It is designed to make learning fun and applicable to daily life!

How Have Women Faced STEM

Women have faced many challenges in STEM fields. Historically, there were fewer women in these areas because society often believed that boys were better suited for STEM. Even today, women sometimes experience unfair treatment and feel left out in workplaces dominated by men. Many organizations are working to change this by encouraging girls to study STEM, providing support and mentorship, and creating more welcoming environments. While things are getting better, it’s important to keep pushing for equal opportunities so that everyone, regardless of gender, can succeed in STEM.

Women in STEM

Check out DR. Mae Jemison

Dr. Mae Jemison was the first African American woman in space. She broke down walls and got tons of kids interested in STEM. She studied chemical engineering and medicine, and then she made history on the Space Shuttle Endeavour. After that, she started the Jemison Group, which tries to use space stuff to help society. Dr. Jemison tells young girls to be curious and chase their goals. She always says, “Never be afraid to ask questions.” Her story shows how important it is to be brave and determined if you want to do well in science, tech, engineering, and math.
